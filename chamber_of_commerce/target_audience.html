Target Audience

The target audience for the Iekja City Chamber of Commerce are those who live and work in Ikeja Lagos. With over 90% of households having access to a computer and internet access, this site will be able to help businesses attract customers.

Persona 1

Shola Adebowale is a 42-year-old. He is a fashion designer and owns a small boutique in Ikeja that sells fabrics and toddler fashion. He is interested in the Ikeja City Chamber of Commerce Website as he seeks to get more business in his store. He wants to participate in local trade fairs but has problem getting information on them. He would like to get his name and business out there by connecting with other businesses and members of his community. He would also like to find others in his community who have similar interests.

Persona 2

Dozie and Chidinma Udoh are a young married couple who moved from Anambra, Lagos and have recently settled in Ikeja. Dozie and Chidinma want to open a resturant featuring flavors from back home. With a demographic that supports this type of restaurant venture, they are interested in looking for spaces that will allow them to run the restaurant, but they would also like to have a banquet space that can be rented out. Families are essential in their culture, and gathering in families would make a great business venture if they offer a place for families to rent out. They would like to connect with other business owners in the area as well as teach others in their community about their culture.

Scenarios:

Scenario 1

A businesswoman who is simply browsing the web to find out a little about the costs of the Corperate Affairs and Commission certificate and the "why" of these important documents in the creation of a company.

Scenario 2

Faruq Olayiwola, an engineer with considerable experience in engineering, design and consultancy projects, has decided to create his company in the city and register it as General Manager. His objective begins by generating 18 jobs for a project that includes the construction of oil wells in the southern part of the country. He wants to know, through the page, what are the costs for his commercial registration and what scope it has in the development of the region, in addition to his benefits.


Scenario 3

3 young people, Ogboanoh Michael, Fayehun Ifeoluwa, and Agboola Habeeb, ages 23, 20, and 24 respectively, search through the Chamber of Commerce website for information on how they can be oriented to identify their potential as company with the Entrepreneur Advice program. They want to build their business as Designers for Children's Fashion, and they found out that through the Chamber of Commerce they can receive support and guidance so that their proposal becomes a successful company in this environment as demanding as fashion.


Scenario 4

A student of the professional career of International Business Administration seeks to support one of her degree projects with the specific information provided by the Chamber of Commerce in the areas of Legal, Financial and Tax Advice, and how these help the future entrepreneur. She knows that through the website she will find the lines of contact and the ways to filter the necessary information.


Scenario 5

Two teachers, Ini Edo and Josephina Cane, want to start their own teaching school for children, and need to know, through the local Chamber of Commerce, the requirements to establish their commercial registration and register their project. Part of your questions include the guide on the process to create a Labor and Administrative Company.
